üìù Summary

This is a mean reversion strategy based on Larry Connors' RSI approach using a 3-period RSI with oversold/overbought levels. It enters long positions when RSI drops below 25 while price is above a 150-period SMA trend filter, and allows pyramiding up to 3 contracts on continued weakness.

üéØ Entry Conditions

LONG Entries: ‚Ä¢ First Entry: RSI(3) crosses below 25 AND close > 150-period SMA ‚Ä¢ Second Entry: RSI(3) < 25 AND current contracts > 0 AND current contracts < 3 (pyramiding) SHORT Entries: ‚Ä¢ No short entries in this strategy

üö™ Exit Conditions

Normal Exits: ‚Ä¢ Long Exit: RSI(3) crosses above 85 (sell all contracts on close) ‚Ä¢ Short Cover: RSI(3) < 25 OR close > 150-period SMA (next bar at market) Stop/Target Exits: ‚Ä¢ Stop loss: 5000 in currency per contract ‚Ä¢ Breakeven: 1000 in currency Time Exits: ‚Ä¢ None

üîç Pattern Details Explained

RSI(3): 3-period Relative Strength Index ‚Ä¢ Meaning: Very short-term momentum oscillator ‚Ä¢ Indicates: Oversold below 25, overbought above 85 ‚Ä¢ Trading use: Mean reversion signals with quick reversals Average(close, 150): 150-period Simple Moving Average ‚Ä¢ Meaning: Long-term trend filter ‚Ä¢ Indicates: Overall market direction ‚Ä¢ Trading use: Only take long trades above this level (trend filter) No PatternFast functions used in this strategy.
